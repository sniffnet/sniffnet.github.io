Sniffnet just reached 500k total downloads!<br><br>
When I started developing Sniffnet almost 4 years ago, 
I’d have never imagined that a project born almost for fun could one day be installed 
half a million times by people from all over the world.<br>
While this might not seem a huge number compared to some enterprise applications, 
from my point of view this is definitely a milestone worth celebrating,
especially considered that I’ve built Sniffnet from scratch as a solo maintainer,
and that the last period accounts for a great fraction of the downloads surge
(more than 200k in the past 10 months).<br>
I see each download as a person my tool helped to debug and better understand 
his/her own network, and this is something that couldn’t make me happier.<br><br>
Even if it’s just a number, I’m so proud of this milestone
and was counting down for it to come for the past few weeks (yeah, I love numbers).<br>
But now let’s cut the chatter and see some some more in-depth statistics
(ok, I really love numbers).

<hr>

### Downloads by operating system

As you can see from the chart below,
the largest slice of Sniffnet’s audience (66%) uses Windows,
and this makes sense since the operating system by Microsoft is by far
the most widespread among the end users worldwide.<br>
Linux follows with xx% and macOS stops at yy%.

It’s curious to compare this with os usage stats…

Official download statistics for other os supported by Sniffnet (for example FreeBSD and NetBSD)
aren’t available and that’s why they aren’t represented in the chart.

<hr>

### Downloads by source

Being Sniffnet packaged in multiple ways,
it’s also interesting to look at the source of the installs.

In this regard, GitHub releases is the clear winner with more than 90% of the share.
This isn’t a surprise since it’s the standard and recommended way to install Sniffnet,
and direct links to the GitHub-hosted executables are prominently 
highlighted both on this website and the project README.<br>
Despite this, there’s a considerable fraction of users that prefer
the Rust package manager crates.io (::%), and the macOS package manager Homebrew (;;%). <br>
The tiniest fraction (::%) comes from the Docker image
I published less than one year ago that is only compatible with Linux.

<hr>

### Looking forward

Sniffnet won’t stop here and I already can’t wait for the 1 million mark!<br>
Let’s keep the snowball rolling and make network monitoring comfortable for even more people!
