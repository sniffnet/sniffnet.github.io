Here we go with a brand refresh for Sniffnet!<br><br>
Exactly 3 years ago, this website was first published under the domain name <code>sniffnet.net</code>. <br>
The domain was chosen in a hurry, and as time passed I grew more and more dissatisfied with it,
especially because of the <i>"net"</i> repeating in the app name and TLD.<br><br>
Additionally, I originally acquired the domain without enabling the mail service, which made it impossible to have official emails for the project. <br>
I could've just updated the old plan to add emails, but I took the opportunity to also change the domain name to <code>sniffnet.app</code>, which is more fitting for a software project and sounds less awkward. <br>
As a nice bonus, the <code>.app</code> TLD is part of the HSTS preload list, meaning browsers enforce a secure HTTPS connection by default: perfectly in line with Sniffnet's commitment to security and privacy.

The old domain still works and redirects to the new one, to avoid breaking existing links and killing SEO. <br>
In the past few days I've done my best to make the transition as smooth as possible, updating all the backlinks I control and requesting reindexing from the most popular search engines to optimize the process. <br>
Obviously I'll keep <code>sniffnet.net</code> indefinitely reserved, to guarantee continuity and prevent impersonation and cybersquatting.

Along with the new domain, I finally set up <b>3 official mailboxes</b>:
- <code>info@sniffnet.app</code> for general inquiries
- <code>security@sniffnet.app</code> for security-related matters and vulnerability reports
- <code>sponsor@sniffnet.app</code> for sponsor inquiries and partnership opportunities

This is a meaningful step-up for the project, providing a more professional and trustworthy image to users and potential partners. <br>
In particular, I believe that having a dedicated email for security is crucial for a project like Sniffnet, as it encourages responsible disclosure of vulnerabilities and complements the existing <a target="_blank" rel="noopener" href="https://github.com/GyulyVGC/sniffnet/blob/main/SECURITY.md">security policy</a> well.

That said, <a target="_blank" rel="noopener" href="https://github.com/GyulyVGC/sniffnet/issues">GitHub Issues</a> remains the preferred channel for any kind of report, suggestion or support request that can be discussed publicly for the benefit of the whole community.
